Breakfast foods to eat and avoid<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

Many studies on breakfast foods have one over-arching theme: what you eat for your morning meal is the most important. Sugary cereals, pastries, bagels, along with high fat, processed meats tend to be popular breakfast food items.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Most of these items contain refined grains and have added sugar. This could cause a rapid blood sugar spike, leading to mid-morning hunger. They also can contain a lot of extra sodium, which could affect blood pressure.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Research shows that having a high-fiber breakfast along with a good amount of protein and some heart-healthy fats leads to better health.<\/p>\n<\/div><\/div>\n\n\n\n
